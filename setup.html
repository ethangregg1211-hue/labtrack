New User Setup Guide

Follow these steps to get LabTrack set up on your phone or computer. It takes about 10 minutes.

1
Open the App
LabTrack runs entirely in your browser — no download or installation needed.
  1. 1
    Open any browser on your phone or computer and go to the LabTrack URL:
    🔗 ethangregg1211-hue.github.io/labtrack
  2. 2
    The app loads instantly. No account, no login, no download required. Your data stays on your device in your browser's local storage.
2
Add to Your iPhone Home Screen
Optional, but recommended — makes it feel and behave like a native app.
  1. 1
    Open the LabTrack URL in Safari (this must be Safari — Chrome does not support adding to home screen on iPhone).
  2. 2
    Tap the Share button — the box with an arrow pointing up at the bottom of the screen.
  3. 3
    Scroll down in the Share menu and tap "Add to Home Screen."
  4. 4
    Name it LabTrack and tap Add in the top right corner.
  5. 5
    The LabTrack icon will appear on your home screen. Tap it to open the app fullscreen, just like a native app.
💡
Once added to your home screen, LabTrack opens without the browser address bar and feels completely native.
3
Create Your EmailJS Account
LabTrack uses EmailJS to send order request emails directly from your browser — no server or backend needed.
  1. 1
    Go to emailjs.com and click Sign Up.
    🔗 emailjs.com
  2. 2
    Create a free account. The free plan allows 200 emails per month and requires no credit card.
  3. 3
    Check your inbox and verify your email address to activate your account.
4
Connect Your Email — Service ID
This links your Gmail or other email account so EmailJS can send from it.
  1. 1
    In the EmailJS dashboard, click "Email Services" in the left sidebar.
  2. 2
    Click "Add New Service."
  3. 3
    Choose Gmail (or your email provider).
  4. 4
    Click "Connect Account" and sign in with your email address.
  5. 5
    Click "Create Service."
  6. 6
    Your service will appear in the list. Copy the Service ID — it looks like: service_xxxxxxx
Your Service ID is shown under Email Services
⬆ Copy your own Service ID from this page — it starts with "service_"
5
Create an Email Template — Template ID
The template tells EmailJS what the order email should look like.
  1. 1
    Click "Email Templates" in the left sidebar.
  2. 2
    Click "Create New Template."
  3. 3
    Set the Subject to: Lab Order Request - {{lab_name}}
  4. 4
    In the Body, delete everything and type only: {{order_list}}
  5. 5
    Click Save.
  6. 6
    Copy the Template ID shown on the template card — it looks like: template_xxxxxxx
⚠️
When entering your Template ID into LabTrack, paste the full ID including "template_" at the start. Do not remove the prefix.
Your Template ID is shown on the Email Templates page
⬆ Copy your own Template ID — it starts with "template_"
6
Get Your Public Key
The Public Key authenticates your browser with EmailJS when sending.
  1. 1
    Click "Account" in the left EmailJS sidebar.
  2. 2
    Your Public Key is shown under "API Keys."
  3. 3
    Copy it. It looks like a random string of letters and numbers, about 20 characters long.
Your Public Key is found under Account › API Keys
⬆ Copy your own Public Key — do not share this with anyone
7
Enter Your Credentials into LabTrack
Paste the three values you copied from EmailJS into the LabTrack Settings page.
  1. 1
    Open LabTrack and tap Settings (the gear icon in the bottom tab bar or sidebar).
  2. 2
    Enter your Lab Name and Researcher Name.
  3. 3
    Enter the email address that order requests should be sent to.
  4. 4
    Paste your Service ID (starts with service_) into the Service ID field.
  5. 5
    Paste your full Template ID (starts with template_) into the Template ID field.
  6. 6
    Paste your Public Key into the Public Key field.
  7. 7
    Tap Save Settings.
🔒
Your credentials are saved only on your device. They are never sent to any server other than EmailJS when you send an order.
8
Import Your Inventory from Excel
Optional — you can add items manually or bulk-import from a spreadsheet.
  1. 1
    Go to Settings and scroll down to "Import Inventory."
  2. 2
    Tap the upload area and select your .xlsx or .csv file.
  3. 3
    A column mapping screen will appear. Match your spreadsheet's column headers to the correct fields:
    Item Name Company Catalog # Notes Preset Quantity Preset Unit
  4. 4
    Tap Continue to review the preview, then Import to add all items at once.
💡
Preset Quantity and Preset Unit are optional columns. If included, they pre-fill the order quantity and unit each time an item is pushed to the reorder queue.
9
Send a Test Order
Confirm everything is working end-to-end before relying on LabTrack for real orders.
  1. 1
    Go to the Inventory tab and tap + Add to create a test item.
  2. 2
    Tap the item to select it, then tap "Push to Reorder" to add it to your reorder queue.
  3. 3
    Go to the Reorder tab, adjust the quantity if needed, then tap "Send Order Request."
  4. 4
    Review the order preview and tap Send.
  5. 5
    Check your email inbox — the order request should arrive within a few seconds. If it doesn't, double-check your credentials in Settings.
✅
If the email arrives, LabTrack is fully set up and ready to use. You can delete the test item from the Inventory tab.
